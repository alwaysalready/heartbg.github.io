ABOUT

I ♥ BETHNAL GREEN is the online home of Save Our Safer Streets, a group of Bethnal Green residents who want to keep and improve the newly built pedestrian-friendly streets around Old Bethnal Green Road, Columbia Road and Arnold Circus.

Our much-loved new, safe streets and community spaces are being threatened with removal by Tower Hamlets Council, who ran two consultations to take out the £2 million of changes which were implemented in 2021.

Beyond the immediate campaign to save our new streets, parklets and community spaces, we will lobby the council to engage with all the residents in Bethnal Green to explore ways to make our public spaces better. Everyone wants to improve air quality and create safer streets for people to walk, talk and play on.

ACTION

The consultation results are out

We must act now!

Over 58% of local residents voted to keep the new street layouts in Bethnal Green, Columbia Road and Arnold Circus! Despite this, the council are proposing a new "Option 3" which still implements most of the rip-outs of Option 1, even though this has been rejected twice by the public. The Mayor will decide on this on Wednesday 20 September at the cabinet meeting.

We’re calling on everyone to join a protest outside the Tower Hamlets Town Hall to say loud and clear that we want to keep the improved road layouts. We don’t want a proposal which will bring back traffic to our roads and which was put together behind closed doors. Please join us at 4:45pm, 20 September, outside Tower Hamlets Town Hall, and please help us spread the word!

Please also write to the Mayor and to councillors before September 20th to voice your views. We know councillors carefully monitor their emails before a major decision, so please do not hesitate to write! For guidance on what to write, who to send it to and an easy to use template, click the button below!

In the next few weeks the council will be deciding what to do: go ahead with their promise to ‘re-open the roads’ or keep our safer streets. We need you to contact the Mayor and your local councillors to keep the pressure on to keep and improve the schemes. All the information you need to do this is here.
