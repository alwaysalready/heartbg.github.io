Contact the Mayor and your councillor… before 20th September

We need as many people as possible to contact the Mayor and local councillors before they decide whether to remove or save our safer streets at a meeting on 20th September. We know they will be checking how many people contact them and what we all say before then.

If you're able to write an email in your own words please see this page.

If you've not got the time to write an email, we've written a template message that you can send directly to the council. Either:

Click on this link to open a pre-populated email message

or

use the content below and paste the email addresses into the respective "To:" and "cc:" fields.

Message content

I am a Tower Hamlets resident and I want you to back option 2 to keep the roads as they are in Bethnal Green at the Cabinet meeting this Wednesday. We residents have now requested option 2 three times. The last thing this area needs is more big changes. We are used to things as they are and we like it.

Air quality is even better within the safer streets area and on the boundary roads than in Tower Hamlets as a whole. This protects kids’ lungs. Barts hospital, local GPs and public health professionals want us to keep the streets as they are - for the sake of our health.

The police want the current street designs to stay as they have reduced anti-social behaviour and improve road safety. The council’s own documents say walking and cycling are safer now, which are the cheapest forms of transport in a cost of living crisis. The council’s own document says that young people will pay the price of any changes to our streets.

Please use the council’s funds for council services that desperately need funding like housing, schools and the cost of living.

Please be a listening council and listen to your residents. None of us have been asked about option 3. Go with what we have told you 3 times now - back option 2 and if you need to make small changes then consult us properly about them and listen to what we say.
